EIA Natural Gas Storage Forecast

February 14, 2026

Overview

A SARIMA time series model for analyzing and forecasting weekly U.S. natural gas storage data from the Energy Information Administration (EIA).

Data

  • EIA Weekly Underground Natural Gas Storage Report
  • Time range: 2008–2026
  • Updated every Thursday

Data Overview

Model

  • SARIMA(p,d,q)(P,D,Q,s) seasonal autoregressive moving average model
  • Weekly AR: current value influenced by the past 5 weeks (\(p = 5\))
  • Weekly MA: current error influenced by the past 5 weeks of errors (\(q = 5\))
  • Annual AR: current value influenced by the same period last year (\(P = 2\)), with annual differencing (\(D = 1\))
  • Annual MA: current error influenced by the same period in the past two years (\(Q = 2\))

Training Parameters

Results

1. Forecast Performance

  • SARIMA(2,0,1)(2,1,1,52) achieved an RMSE of 44.85 on a 104-week test set
  • MAPE of 1.8%, indicating high forecast accuracy
  • 35% reduction in forecast error versus a naive seasonal baseline
  • Residuals show no significant autocorrelation

2. Seasonality

  • Natural gas storage follows a clear 52-week annual cycle
  • Injection season (Apr–Oct) and withdrawal season (Nov–Mar) transition points are accurately captured
  • Storage levels at lag 26 weeks show a -0.5 correlation with current levels

3. Model Notes

  • Seasonal differencing significantly improved accuracy in practice, despite the ADF test p-value being below 0.05
  • Higher-order AR/MA terms (p, q > 2) led to overfitting and worse test set performance

Applications

  • Energy Trading: 2–4 week storage forecasts can inform natural gas futures strategies
  • Risk Management: Prediction intervals quantify uncertainty for supply chain decisions
  • Market Analysis: Flags anomalous storage levels as early warning signals for supply/demand imbalances
